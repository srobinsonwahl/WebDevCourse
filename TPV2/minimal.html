Minimal Techno.


Bare, catchy, new.

Minimal techno is a subgenre of techno characterized by a bare-bones style
with simplistic beats and rhythms. This genre originated from Detroit in the
early 1990s, eventually popularized in Germany by well-known Djs and labels
big in the club scene. Minimal came as a second wave of techno out of Detroit
after Detroit techno left the city and moved abroad into Germany and other eastern
European areas. Picture your common electronic techno music. Now take off
the rash, hard-sounds, and slow down the intensity of everything, and there
you will have Minimal techno. It is a raw sound, with simple drums, bare
basslines, and only essential sounds to move you with the music. Minimal
is all about the rhythm that you can produce with low amounts of distortion,
and a clean sound.
Minimal uses two specific stylistic appraoches: sleletalism and massification.
In skeletal minimal techno, only the core elements of the sound are included
and the only variance in noises is kept at a bare minimum to keep interest in the
songs. Massification is a style of minimalism in which many sounds are layered
over time, but with little variation in sonic elements. Minimal techno's sound
is much more fit for the mainstream music industry where many songs are sampled
and chopped to become background to heavier pop songs. The average tempo of a
minimal techno track is between 125 and 130 beats per minute, and focuses on
middle frequencies rather than deep basses to drive the listener towards euphoria.
Minimal is cool, it's hip, but it's not what I am looking for out of techno music.
I am a listener that loves depth to sounds, lots of distortion, and noises that can
peak my interest to listen harder. Minimal's bare-bones approach doesn't offer me
those characteristics. In my opinion, the only thing this subgenre offers me that I
enjoy is the clinical use of rhtyhm as the main driver for each song. I appreciate
the science behind creating such catchy songs with little elements to them, but I can't
listen to a genre solely for that reason. If you are against all of the experimental
samples and sounds that are found in other genres, and you want more of a mellow vibe,
minimal is definitely for you. Give it a listen, figure out if it's for you, and tell
your friends about Techno Gyár so they can explore other genres with you!
