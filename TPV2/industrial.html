Industrial Techno.


Gritty, dark, and hard-hitting.

Industrial techno is a dark, hard-hitting style of Techno
that incorporates rhythms and parallels with the sounds and
settings of factories with machinery. The subgenre originated
in the 1990s, with key artists such as Cut Hands, Helena Hauff,
Surgeon, and many more growing the style to a higher popularity.
Industrial techno has a grittier, complex and layered sound
with an extremely experimental side to it. Artists layer tracks
with obscure machinery noises and fast-paced, bleak baselines
to produce a foggy aura that takes you to the 19th century factory
floor. Instead of putting cogs on a rail, you will be jumping,
waving, yelling, spinning, stepping, and bobbing: All. Night. Long.
It's noisy, less melodic, a ton of distortoin, and generally
much faster than any electronic music you'll see when you're out.
One could even describe the sound as "pots and pans"; we certainly
think it's much deeper than that. Techno Industrial incoporates
metallic, machine-like symphonies and other sounds that aren't
typically used in mainstream techno. The subgenre coins the term
"industrial" because artists create tracks with the objective of
creating something that reflects the industrial world, an approach
that isn't seen in any other genres. Lots of clanks, lots of abrupt
banging, and distorted sampling methods combine into an extremely
rash, mind-numbing sound; a sound we call Industrial Techno.

While I enjoy listening and exploring all kinds of genres of music
I do not work in this field, I do not know how to create these sounds,
I am just a consumer; a college-student consumer who likes electronic

abrasive sounds make it difficult for me to really tune in to the music.
It's hard for me to follow a track from start to finish when every few
seconds there is a sampled drill-sound being blasted into my ears.
Although I understand this sound has had a ton of glory in the past,
it's not my taste. I prefer more melodic, transe-like sounds that
flow well across the track. Industrial techno doesn't offer me that
