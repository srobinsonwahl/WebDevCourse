Tech House.


Metallic, light, dance.

Tech house is a subgenre of electronic music that mixes stylistic features of techno with house music. In general, this style combines musical aspets of techno like edgy baselines and metallic beats with the grooves of house. It is a lighter style of music with less harsh beats and more funky dance harmonies. The music originally had a clean and minimal style that originated with techno from Detroit and the UK. It's jazzy, captivating, and a vibe that could be seen as tranquil in the world of techno. Since the early 2000's tech house has spread in Europe, with major influence in the dance scene of Spain.
As a mixture of multiple genres, tech house's sound has to be a proper balance of deep techno baselines layered underneath a melodic house hook. Elements of the house sound such as realistic jazz-sounds and booming kick drums are replaced with techno's deep and distorted kicks, noiser snares, and more synth melodies. The sound is a middle ground between the two genres, where techno's industrial tones are smoothed over with house's colorful and peaceful tones. Some songs offer soulful vocals on top of the funky beats to create a more peaceful sound. Tech house requires a mastery of balance.
Tech house is more of my type. I like the upbeat attitude that this subgenre brings forward. Outside of techno, I do enjoy listening to house music so I think naturally this genre just makes sense to me. Replacing house's jazz-sounds with techno's brutal baselines and drums creates a sound that resonates well with me. It's a beautiful take on combining two subgenres that mix well together, and I am excited to see how much this genre will grow. Eventually, tech house could become popular enough for mainstream radio play as more and more people shift away from hip-hop and into the electronic music scene.
