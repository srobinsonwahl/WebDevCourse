Detroit Techno.


Funky, futuristic, refined.

Detroit techno is a subgenre of techno music that began from notable productions by Detroit-based artists during the 1980s. Detroit techno pieces can be classified by their futuristic sound along with a funky and classy tone that carries throughout songs. This genre was made popular in middle-class neighborhoods in Detroit where a new electronic scene was creating a divide in the party atmosphere of the city. One side of the city was listening to hip-hop and rap while the other was diving into European-influenced techno music. This new age of music for the city eventually led to the creation of Detroit's own sound; one of high-tech-sounding beats accompanied by futuristic themes.
The sound embodies a mixture of European techno instruments, Chicago-house influence, and an Afro-futuristic tone. Synths and drum machines are heavily used in each song, creating an ultra-electro twang that carries the genre. House-music's steady 4/4 beats were carried over into the Detroit sound, with danceable tempos that let people really enjoy their listening. Sampling from science-fiction movies and other utopian noises are incorporated into each song, all the while maintaining a Black American style of music. Sonically, Detroit techno music combines the cool, detached dancefloor beats and textures of European electronic music with the soul and celebration of American funk music.
While the story of Detroit techno is respectable and interesting, I personally don't enjoy listening to this genre. It's too futuristic for my liking and I find the spake-like sounds to be annoying to listen to. After hearing a few hours of many techno subgenres, I wasn't too keen on Detroit's sound. I enjoy listening to darker, hypnotic sounds that carry over deep baselines, and Detroit techno doesn't offer that. It's unique, it's funky, and you must try out this subgenre if you want to feel like you're listening to the mainstream of 2080.
