Minimal Techno.


Bare, catchy, new.

Minimal techno is a subgenre of techno characterized by a bare-bones style with simplistic beats and rhythms. This genre originated from Detroit in the early 1990s, eventually popularized in Germany by well-known Djs and labels big in the club scene. Minimal came as a second wave of techno out of Detroit after Detroit techno left the city and moved abroad into Germany and other eastern European areas. Picture your common electronic techno music. Now take off the rash, hard-sounds, and slow down the intensity of everything, and there you will have Minimal techno. It is a raw sound, with simple drums, bare basslines, and only essential sounds to move you with the music. Minimal is all about the rhythm that you can produce with low amounts of distortion, and a clean sound.
Minimal uses two specific stylistic appraoches: sleletalism and massification. In skeletal minimal techno, only the core elements of the sound are included and the only variance in noises is kept at a bare minimum to keep interest in the songs. Massification is a style of minimalism in which many sounds are layered over time, but with little variation in sonic elements. Minimal techno's sound is much more fit for the mainstream music industry where many songs are sampled and chopped to become background to heavier pop songs. The average tempo of a minimal techno track is between 125 and 130 beats per minute, and focuses on middle frequencies rather than deep basses to drive the listener towards euphoria.
