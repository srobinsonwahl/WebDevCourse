Trance is a genre of electronic dance music that came out of the combination of
Britsh new-age music and 1990s German techno. It's a musical form that distinctly
builds tension and elements throughout a track that's usually made up of a few peaks,
or drops. Although people generally classify Trance as its own genre of music, because
of techno's influence on the creation of the genre, it can be considered its own category
of techno. The genre is named after the state that the music is aimed to induce onto its
listeners. A trance is a state of hypnotism and heightened consciousness, and Trance techno
surely does bring that onto listeners. This is accomplished by the mixing of layers with
with distinctly foreshadowed build-up and drops; a constant cycle that intrigues listeners
to truly listen, and feel the music.
Trance is like looking down a 50 floor stairwell, where you are upside down, floating
down between each level rotating 360 degrees as you descend. It's a hynotic form of
music that captures all of your attention and rewards you with a heightened mood and
an elevated envrionment. A kick drum is usually placed on every downbeat, and a regular
open hi-hat is often placed on the upbeat. Major transitions, builds, and rhythmic
climaxes are foreshadowed prior to their appearance, and distorted vocals overlay an
atmosphere of pure hynotism. Recent forms of Trance techno incorporate other styles and
elements of electronic music like "Electro" or progressive house into different parts
of each song. Djs blend together fading inward and outward sounds to keep a constant track
going for listeners, and Trance's confusing, yet predictable nature allows incredibly
satisfying transitions between songs.
It's a riveting form of music. I wholeheartedly enjoy Trance for exactly what it is; a
beautiful form of music that is so powerful it will physically take you in with it. Trance
has such a unique sound and composition compared to other forms of techno because it forces
producers to really be creative, and feel how the music flows for their audience. On the spot
a performer can switch up the entire atmosphere of their crowd with the simple fade of one sound
into another. Listeners expect these changes and prepare themselves, ultimately attaching them even
more to the music. I love listening to Trance. It has all of the great elements of classic German
